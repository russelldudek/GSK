Dear GSK Hiring Team,

It probably feels like the AI opportunity in Market Access is obvious, while the execution risk is harder: too many promising use cases, too many stakeholders with legitimate concerns, and too many pilots that can look impressive without changing a payer strategy, workflow, decision or launch outcome. That is the problem that draws me to the Director, Agentic AI Solutions - US Market Access role.

My background is not the conventional path of a career Market Access leader, and I would not ask you to overlook that. My case is different: I have spent more than twenty years translating complex technical systems into governed operating practice across AI, manufacturing, robotics, logistics, customer operations and high-consequence environments. I am strongest where business leaders, domain experts, Tech, Legal, Privacy, Compliance and external partners need a shared portfolio, clear decision rights, practical delivery plans and evidence that adoption is producing value.

At Vape-Jet, I lead operations at the intersection of machine-vision automation, AI-first transformation, production, customer support, engineering issue flow, ERP/Jira workflows and a field fleet of approximately 1,000 robots. The work is less about building a clever model than building the operating system around it: define the problem, make signals visible, prioritize the work, establish human review and escalation, coordinate technical owners, train users and measure whether the workflow actually improves. Through DudeWorth and DudexOS, I also build and advise on agentic workflows using structured context, goal loops, retrieval patterns, AI agents, governance rails and human judgment.

The GSK role is compelling because it sits in that same connective tissue. I would approach the US Market Access AI portfolio with disciplined discovery and value targeting: map decisions and workflows with the people doing the work; score opportunities by business value, feasibility, data readiness, risk, adoption burden and time-to-proof; define explicit human decision rights and compliance controls; coordinate Tech and vendors through clear delivery briefs; instrument benefits before scale; and design training, role changes and feedback loops as part of the product rather than an afterthought.

My scientific background strengthens the translation layer. My University of Pittsburgh studies gave me grounding in materials science, physical chemistry and biology, including minor studies in biology. Earlier technical work around advanced electronics, semiconductor-adjacent materials and high-reliability systems taught me to respect scientific evidence, boundary conditions and failure modes. That foundation helps me engage credibly with technical experts while keeping the business question legible: what decision are we improving, what evidence supports it, what risk must be controlled, and what outcome will tell us the change worked?

GSK's culture—ambitious for patients, accountable for impact and doing the right thing—is especially resonant for agentic AI. Autonomous systems in a regulated health environment need more than enthusiasm. They need bounded authority, approved data use, traceable actions, monitoring, clear escalation, and leaders willing to stop or redesign a solution when the evidence does not support scale. My operating style is to build those mechanisms early so teams can move faster because trust has been designed into the workflow.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my combination of AI product thinking, operating-system design, cross-functional leadership, change adoption and science-grounded technical empathy could help GSK turn a portfolio of Agentic AI opportunities into measurable Market Access outcomes.
